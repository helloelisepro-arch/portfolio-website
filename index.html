Built an SEO content programme from scratch for Red Points, a B2B SaaS brand protection platform targeting IP managers and legal teams. Starting with keyword research via Screaming Frog and SEMrush, I identified high-intent topics across trademark enforcement, brand protection, and IP law.

Produced ~40 long-form articles — written to rank and written to be read. The work contributed to 500% YoY organic traffic growth. One article on Amazon's fake review problem gained enough traction that Amazon's Head of Brand called Red Points directly to request it be taken down.

Articles remain indexed and ranking today.

CV · April 2026

I've spent the last 8 years working across three languages, in B2B and B2C environments, helping turn complex ideas into something people can understand and use.

The majority of that work sat in areas where clarity really matters, from IP law and data privacy to creative education. Not just simplifying for the sake of it, but making sure the message holds up when people need to act on it.

I've led international campaigns running in up to eight languages, working closely with growth teams, designers, copywriters and localisation teams to get everything aligned. What I care about in those moments is not just the output, but the level of precision behind it.

When the message is right, everything else moves faster.

I'm comfortable in environments where the stakes are high and decisions need to be made. I know what good looks like, and I'm not afraid to say when something isn't there yet.

A few years ago, AI started changing the shape of content work in a very real way — including my own role. It pushed me to step back and be more intentional about where I bring value.

Today, I focus on the strategic side, where creative instinct, structure, and emerging tools come together. I'm interested in how teams can move faster, stay consistent, and make better decisions as the way we produce content continues to evolve.
